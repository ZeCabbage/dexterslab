# Security and Defense

## Protecting Your Homestead and Community

In a grid-down or collapse scenario, security becomes essential. The goal is deterrence, detection, and defense in that order. Avoid conflict whenever possible — even a minor injury without access to medical care can be life-threatening.

## Situational Awareness

### OODA Loop
The decision-making framework used by military and law enforcement:
- **Observe**: Continuously scan your environment for changes, threats, and opportunities
- **Orient**: Interpret what you observe based on context and experience
- **Decide**: Choose the best course of action
- **Act**: Execute your decision quickly and decisively

Practice recognizing the baseline of normal activity in your area. When something deviates from the baseline, investigate.

## Layered Perimeter Security

### Outer Perimeter — Early Warning (500+ feet from shelter)
- Trip wires connected to noise makers (tin cans with pebbles, bells, or wind chimes)
- Motion-activated solar-powered lights
- Dogs are one of the most effective early warning systems — they detect approach long before humans
- Natural barriers: thorny hedgerows (Osage orange, hawthorn, multiflora rose), dense brush, ditches, fencing

### Middle Perimeter — Detection (100-500 feet)
- Clear sight lines: remove brush and vegetation that could conceal an approach
- Gravel paths around structures (footsteps on gravel are very audible)
- Windows positioned and shaded to allow you to see out without being easily seen
- Observation points at elevated positions

### Inner Perimeter — Hardened (the structure itself)
- Reinforced doors with solid deadbolts. Consider barricade brackets
- Window security: bars, shutters, or reinforced glazing. Cover with heavy curtains at night to prevent light leak
- Safe room: one interior room with reinforced door, communication equipment, water, first aid, and emergency supplies
- Multiple exit points — never allow yourself to be trapped with only one way out

## Concealment and Light Discipline

In a grid-down scenario, a lit house at night is visible for miles and advertises that someone is home with resources.

- Use blackout curtains on all windows
- Cook during daylight hours when possible — cooking fires at night create visible light and aromatic smoke
- Small headlamps with red-light mode preserve night vision and are less visible than white light
- If you must have fire at night, use a Dakota fire hole (dig a pit with an air channel — contains light and reduces smoke)
- Reduce noise: avoid generators at night, speak in lowered voices, secure loose items that clang in wind
- Vary your routine — predictable patterns make you vulnerable

## Defensive Positioning

- High ground provides observation advantage and makes approach more difficult
- Identify natural cover positions around your property (stone walls, earth berms, thick trees, ditches)
- Cover stops bullets. Concealment hides you but does not stop bullets. Know the difference
- Plan multiple defensive positions so you can relocate if one is compromised
- Always have at least two escape routes from any position

## Community Organization

In any extended grid-down scenario, groups survive better than individuals.

### Role Assignment
Assign community members based on skills and fitness:
- **Security/watch**: Rotating shifts, minimum two people per shift
- **Medical**: Someone with first aid training or medical background
- **Food production**: Gardening, cooking, preservation specialists
- **Communications**: Radio operator
- **Maintenance**: Repairs, construction, water systems
- **Education/childcare**: Keeping children safe and education continuing

### Watch Schedule
- Divide 24 hours into 4-6 hour shifts
- Never have a single person on watch — minimum two prevents sleep and creates accountability
- Establish communication protocols: check-in signals, alarm signals (three quick whistle blasts, bell ringing, etc.)
- Pre-establish rally points where the group assembles if alarm is raised

## Conflict De-escalation

Fighting should be an absolute last resort. The goal is always to avoid, deter, or de-escalate.

- Project strength without aggression: security measures visible from outside, organized appearance, communication capability
- Trade and cooperation are more valuable than confrontation
- Never reveal the full extent of your supplies or capabilities
- Keep interactions at a distance when possible — physical separation reduces tension
- If approached by strangers: send a small group to meet them well away from your main camp/home
- Have a designated spokesperson — multiple people talking creates confusion and perceived weakness

## Travel Security

If you must move between locations:

- Travel at dawn or dusk when visibility is reduced but navigation is still possible
- Stay off main roads — these are the most likely locations for ambush and crowd movement
- Move in groups of 2-4 people minimum. Solo travel is highest risk
- Designate a point person (front), rear guard, and communicator
- Leave a trip plan with someone at your base: route, expected arrival time, and fallback plan if you do not return
- Carry communication equipment (radio) to call for assistance if needed
- Vary your routes — never travel the same path twice if possible
- Be aware of choke points (bridges, narrow paths, overpasses) — pass through quickly and with caution
