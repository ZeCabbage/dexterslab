# Water Purification

## How to Purify Water in a Survival Situation

Water purification is the most critical survival skill. Contaminated water causes cholera, dysentery, and death faster than starvation. Always purify water before drinking regardless of how clean it appears.

## Finding Water Sources

Look for flowing water over stagnant water. Prioritize in this order:
- Springs and seeps emerging from ground (cleanest natural source)
- Fast-moving streams and rivers
- Lakes and ponds (filter sediment first)
- Rain collection (cleanest if collected properly)
- Snow and ice (must melt before drinking — eating snow lowers body temperature)
- Morning dew collected with cloth dragged through grass
- Solar still: dig hole, place container in center, cover with clear plastic, place small rock over container to create drip point
- Plant transpiration bag: tie clear plastic bag around leafy branch, water collects as plant transpires

## Boiling Water — The Gold Standard

Boiling is the most reliable purification method. It kills all bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

**Procedure:**
1. Filter cloudy water through clean cloth, bandana, or coffee filter to remove sediment
2. Bring water to a rapid, rolling boil
3. Maintain the boil for at least 1 minute
4. At altitudes above 6,500 feet (2,000 meters), boil for 3 minutes
5. Allow to cool naturally before drinking
6. If the water tastes flat, pour back and forth between two clean containers to aerate

## Chemical Purification with Household Bleach

Use only regular unscented household liquid bleach containing 5-8.25% sodium hypochlorite. Do NOT use scented, color-safe, or bleach with added cleaners.

**Bleach ratios for water purification:**

For 5-6% sodium hypochlorite bleach:
- Clear water: 8 drops per gallon (2 drops per liter)
- Cloudy or cold water: 16 drops per gallon (4 drops per liter)

For 8.25% sodium hypochlorite bleach:
- Clear water: 6 drops per gallon
- Cloudy or cold water: 12 drops per gallon

**Instructions:**
1. Mix bleach into water thoroughly
2. Let stand for 30 minutes minimum
3. Water should have a faint chlorine smell after 30 minutes
4. If no chlorine smell, repeat the dosage and wait another 15 minutes
5. If still no chlorine smell after second treatment, the water is too contaminated — find another source

## Chemical Purification with Iodine

Tincture of iodine (2%) from a first aid kit works for short-term emergency disinfection.

- Clear water: 5 drops per quart or liter
- Cloudy water: 10 drops per quart or liter
- Stir well and let stand 30 minutes minimum
- In cold water below 40°F, double the wait time to 60 minutes

**Caution:** Do not use iodine treatment long-term. Pregnant women and people with thyroid conditions should avoid iodine-treated water.

## Solar Disinfection (SODIS Method)

Uses ultraviolet radiation from sunlight to kill pathogens. Works only with clear water.

1. Use a clear transparent plastic bottle (PET plastic — check for recycling symbol #1)
2. Filter water to remove all visible particles
3. Fill bottle three-quarters full, shake vigorously for 20 seconds to oxygenate, then fill completely
4. Place bottle horizontally in direct intense sunlight on a reflective surface (corrugated metal roof is ideal)
5. Expose for 6-8 hours in bright sunny conditions
6. In partly cloudy conditions, expose for 2 full days
7. Do not use this method on rainy or fully overcast days

## DIY Filtration — Important Limitations

A charcoal and sand filter removes sediment, improves taste, and reduces some chemical contaminants. However, it does NOT kill bacteria, viruses, or parasites. You must still boil or chemically treat filtered water.

**Building a gravity filter:**
1. Cut bottom off a plastic bottle or use a container with a hole in the bottom
2. Layer from bottom to top: small pebbles (1 inch), coarse sand (2 inches), crushed charcoal from hardwood fire (3 inches), fine sand (2 inches), cloth or grass on top
3. Pour water through slowly and collect from the bottom
4. The first few batches will be cloudy — discard until water runs clear
5. Replace charcoal layer after 50-100 uses

## Combined Method for Maximum Safety

For the safest water in a survival situation, combine methods:
1. Settle: let murky water sit for 30 minutes so heavy particles sink
2. Filter: pour through cloth or DIY filter to remove remaining sediment
3. Purify: boil for 1 minute OR treat with bleach/iodine at proper ratios
4. Store in clean, covered container

## Water Storage Safety

- Use food-grade containers only
- For long-term storage: add 1/4 teaspoon of unscented bleach per gallon
- Store in cool, dark location
- Rotate stored water every 6-12 months
- Label containers with the date stored

## Warning Signs of Contaminated Water

Never drink water that:
- Has a chemical or petroleum smell
- Has an unnatural color (green algae is treatable, but chemical colors are not)
- Is near industrial sites, mines, or agricultural runoff
- Has dead animals nearby

No purification method removes heavy metals, radioactive particles, or industrial chemicals. If you suspect these contaminants, find a different source entirely.
